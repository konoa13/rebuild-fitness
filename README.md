Rebuild Fitness  Web Design Document
Adam Konopka

Project Overview
Rebuild is a made up personal fitness business. The website helps people learn about the business and sign up for a free call with a trainer. 

PART 1: CONTENT

Q1. What is the name of your business?
Rebuild

Q2. Is the business real or fictional?
It is fictional 

Q3. What products or services does the business offer?

Custom workout plans made for each person
Personal meal plans based on the person's goals and food preferences
A full package that includes both the workout and meal plan plus check-ins
Online coaching that works from anywhere using a phone or computer


Q4. Who is the target audience?
People who want to lose weight, build muscle, or just get healthier. This includes complete beginners and people who have worked out before but need a plan.

Q5. What problem does this business solve?
A lot of people want to get in shape but don't know where to start. They don't know what exercises to do or what to eat. Rebuild gives them a clear plan made just for them so they don't have to figure it out alone.

Q6. What makes this business unique compared to competitors?
Most fitness apps give everyone the same plan. Rebuild makes a different plan for every single person based on their body, goals, and schedule. No two clients get the same plan.

Q7. What pages will your website include?

Home — the first page visitors see, with a headline and a button to get started
Services — shows the three programs the business offers
Contact — a form where visitors can book a free call

Q8. Write a short mission statement for the business.
Our mission is to help people transform their bodies and build healthier lifestyles through personalized training and nutrition guidance.

Q9. What tone should the website content have?
Professional and motivating. The writing is clear and direct. It talks to the visitor personally.

Q10. What call-to-action do you want visitors to take?
Book a free consultation by filling out the contact form. Every button on the site leads to that form.

Q11. What information must be immediately visible on the homepage?

The business name: REBUILD
The main headline: "Transform Your Body."
A short sentence explaining what the business does
A button that says "Start Your Journey"

Q12. Will the site include images, videos, or other media? Why?
Right now the site uses emoji icons to keep things simple. 

PART 2: DESIGN

Q1. What overall style will the website have?
Modern and clean. Dark background, bold text, and a green and blue color theme. Nothing too busy.

Q2. What color scheme will you use and why?
Black because for the main background
Dark Grey used for cards and imput boxes
White Main text easy to read on black background
Gray used for the small text
Green becasue green is used for health
Blue because blue and green go well together

Q3. What fonts will you use for headings and body text?
The font used everywhere is Montserrat

Big headings use the heaviest weight (900) so they stand out
Smaller headings use bold (700)
Regular text uses normal weight (400) so it is easy to read
Buttons and labels use semi-bold (600–700) with extra letter spacing


Q4. How will spacing and layout improve readability?

Each section has a lot of space above and below it so things don't feel crowded
Text paragraphs have a max width so lines don't stretch too far across the screen
Cards and form fields have gaps between them so everything feels organized
The navigation bar stays at the top of the screen at all times

Q5. What visual hierarchy will guide the user's attention?

The big headline is the first thing you see
The green button right under it is the next thing that catches your eye
Small green labels tell you what each new section is about
Bold section titles anchor each part of the page
Cards show the services in a neat grid
The form at the bottom is open and easy to fill out

Q6. How will consistency be maintained across pages?

The same colors are used everywhere
Every section uses the same label and title style
The same font and button style appear throughout
The navigation bar is always visible at the top

Q7. What icons will you use?
Simple emoji icons that everyone's computer can show:

🏋️ for the workout plan
🥗 for the meal plan
⚡ for the full transformation package

Q8. What emotions should the design evoke in users?
motivation,excited

Q9. How will accessibility be considered?

White text on a dark background is easy to read for most people
Font sizes are big enough to read comfortably on any screen
The HTML uses proper tags like nav, section, footer, 
The site works on  computers
All links and buttons can be reached 

Q10. What inspiration sources influenced your design?

Gymshark  — dark theme, bold text, clean look
Xela — minimal fitness style with lots of open space

PART 3: INTERACTIVITY
Q1. What interactive elements will your site include?

A navigation bar at the top with links that scroll to each section
Buttons that take you directly to the contact form
A hamburger menu on mobile that opens and closes the nav links
Hover effects on the service cards  they lift up slightly when you mouse over them
A contact form with text boxes, a dropdown, and a message area
Fade in animations that play as you scroll down the page

Q2. How will users navigate the website?
There is a navigation bar fixed at the top of the screen. It has links to Home, Services, and Contact. 

Q3. Will there be form validation or user feedback? Explain.
Yes. When you click "Send Message" the JavaScript checks three things:

First name must be filled in
Last name must be filled in
Email must look like a real email address like john@gmail.com

Q4. What JavaScript functionality will you implement?
goTo(id) Scrolls the page smoothly to a section
toggleMenu() Opens or closes the mobile menu
closeMenu() Closes the mobile menu when a link is clicked
IntersectionObserver  Makes elements fade in when you scroll to them
submitForm() Checks the form and shows a success or error message

Q5. How does interactivity improve user experience?

Smooth scrolling makes the site feel nicer to use instead of jumping around
Fade in animations make the page feel alive as you scroll
Hover effects show users what they can click on
Form validation stops people from sending an empty or broken form and tells them what to fix
The mobile menu makes the site easy to use on a phone
